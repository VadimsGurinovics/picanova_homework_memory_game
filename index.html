Welcome

Welcome to our Concentration Game

Concentration, also known as Match Match, Match Up, Memory, Pelmanism, Shinkei-suijaku, Pexeso or simply Pairs, is a card game in which all of the cards are laid face down on a surface and two cards are flipped face up over each turn. The object of the game is to turn over pairs of matching cards.

Any deck of playing cards may be used, although there are also commercial sets of cards with images. The rules given here are for a standard deck of 52 cards, which are normally laid face down in four rows of 13 cards each. The two jokers may be included for a total of six rows of nine cards each.

Additional packs can be used for added interest. Standard rules need not be followed: the cards can be spread out anywhere, such as all around a room.

In turn, each player chooses two cards and turns them face up. If they are of the same rank and color (e.g. six of hearts and six of diamonds, queen of clubs and queen of spades, or both jokers, if used) then that player wins the pair and plays again. If they are not of the same rank and color, they are turned face down again and play passes to the player on the left. Rules can be changed here too: it can be agreed before the game starts that matching pairs be any two cards of the same rank, a color-match being unnecessary, or that the match must be both rank and card suit.

The game ends when the last pair has been picked up. The winner is the person with the most pairs. There may be a tie for first place.

Concentration may be played solo either as a leisurely exercise, or with the following scoring method: play as normal, but keep track of the number of non-matching pairs turned over (this may be done using poker chips, pennies or by making marks on a sheet of paper). The object is to clear the tableau in the fewest turns, or to get the lowest possible score. With perfect memorization and using an optimal strategy, the expected number of moves needed for a game with {\displaystyle n}n cards converges to {\displaystyle \approx 0.8*n}{\displaystyle \approx 0.8*n}, with {\displaystyle n\to \infty }n\to \infty .[1] For a standard deck of 52 cards, the expected value is {\displaystyle \approx 41.4}{\displaystyle \approx 41.4} moves.

How to play

The game itself is to be implemented in Javascript (you can use any toolkit you like if it helps you, but the page shouldn't need any server-side logic). The implementation is for one user playing the Concentration game alone. The user can turn cards until he collected all pairs. The game should show a 4x4 grid (16 cards in total, 2x8 of them unique). When the "Game" page is shown, the cards are shown with the face down and are shuffled randomly by the algorithm. The player can then click on a card to turn it and see the image on the card. The user then clicks on the second card to turn also the second card. If both cards show the same image, the cards are taken away from the table and the user gets a point. If the cards show different images, after a timeout, they are turned face-down again. After the user successfully collects all cards, a message is shown with congratulations, informing the user of the number of moves he needed to collect all cards. Then the user can click on a button/link "Start new game" to start the game again.
